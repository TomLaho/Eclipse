INSTRUCTIONS: When summarising, prioritise in this order:
1. Your own commitments — deliverables, deadlines, follow-ups. Flag any that are
   slipping, vague, or undated.
2. Decisions made — what was decided on scope, approach, or prioritisation, and WHY.
3. Action items owned by others — name the owner explicitly. Format every action as:
   owner + action + due date (flag it if no date was given).
4. Risks, blockers, dependencies, and missing information — especially anything that
   stalls progress or threatens a timeline.
5. Commercial implications — cost/revenue impact, assumptions, scope changes,
   stakeholder expectations.
6. Assumptions or items needing future validation.

Surface every vague or undated next step. Pay attention to detail that's easy to
lose in the room: implied commitments, unspoken assumptions, cross-team
dependencies, decisions made without supporting data.

Tone: factual, direct, no flattery. Focus on what changed, what was agreed, and what
happens next. Make ownership explicit; if unclear, call it out.
